Specific Challenges

  • Ensuring the app works like it should on a touch device
  • Adding some amount of 'native' feel to app. 'Not a website...'
  • Keeping a high FPS. The app is the user's sole focus
    • I started with GSAP to do animations and I found there to be quite a bit of jittering lack of performance. My guess as to why, was the he WebGL side of the JS thread was taking a lot of bandwidth. I also did not like the added boilerplate of GSAP. Once I introduced CSSTransitions, animations felt much smoother and were more in line with what design expected.
    • Get my hands on an actual device. Developing this in the electron dev window vs viewing the built app running on a touch device
    • Even though we were building this in Electron which is just a chrome browser, there was an ask by design to add some 'native' touches to the app. So these included little things live swipe actions, touch animations, and scroll behavior. It took some extra effort to find the right tool and implement them properly
